# Glossary

A complete reference for every concept and feature in Atono — 63 entries.

## A

- [Acceptance Criteria](/glossary/acceptance-criteria) — Testable conditions that define when a story is complete, written as a hierarchical list with up to three levels of nesting.
- [Active Sprint](/glossary/active-sprint) — The single sprint a Scrum team is currently executing.
- [Activities](/glossary/activities) — A chronological, filterable log of all changes to a story, bug, epic, or feature flag including title changes, workflow transitions, assignments, comments, attachments, subtasks, and pull requests.
- [Administrator](/glossary/administrator) — A workspace role with full administrative privileges including managing workspace settings, environments, users, integrations, and SSO configuration, but without access to billing.
- [Ask Capy](/glossary/ask-capy) — An AI-powered Q&amp;A tool that searches completed stories and bugs in your workspace to answer product questions.
- [Assignee](/glossary/assignee) — The user responsible for completing a story or bug.
- [Atono API](/glossary/atono-api) — The public REST API for programmatic access to Atono data and operations, authenticated using API Keys.
- [Attachment](/glossary/attachment) — A file added to a story or bug, displayed as thumbnails for images/videos or as file info for other types.
- [Audit Log](/glossary/audit-log) — A chronological, tamper-evident record of significant user and system actions across a workspace, such as user invitations, permission changes, SSO configuration updates, and integration connections.

## B

- [Backlog (Page)](/glossary/backlog-page) — A team page for planning and prioritizing upcoming work before it moves into execution.
- [Blocker](/glossary/blocker) — An impediment preventing a story or bug from progressing through the workflow.
- [Bug](/glossary/bug) — An error, flaw, or unintended behavior in software requiring investigation and resolution.
- [Burndown Chart](/glossary/burndown-chart) — A Scrum team chart tracking remaining story points in a sprint over time, updated as stories are completed or moved out of scope.

## C

- [Comment](/glossary/comment) — A discussion thread on a story or bug, either on the item as a whole or anchored to specific highlighted text or an acceptance criterion.
- [Customer](/glossary/customer) — A named tenant or organization whose users interact with your product, identified by a string value passed to the Atono SDK or Chrome extension.
- [Cycle Time](/glossary/cycle-time) — The total time a story or bug spends in workflow steps assigned to the In Progress category for a specific team.

## D

- [Definition of Done](/glossary/definition-of-done) — A team-wide agreement specifying all conditions a story or bug must satisfy before it can be marked complete.
- [Dependency](/glossary/dependency) — A relationship where one work item cannot be started or completed until another item is finished.
- [Done (Workflow Category)](/glossary/done-workflow-category) — A workflow category containing steps representing completed work.

## E

- [Epic](/glossary/epic) — A planning item that groups related stories under a single shared goal, providing a high-level view of progress across multiple pieces of work.
- [Estimation](/glossary/estimation) — The process of predicting the relative effort required to complete a story or bug, typically expressed in story points using a Fibonacci scale.

## F

- [Feature Flag](/glossary/feature-flag) — A named toggle controlling whether a feature is visible or active in each environment, without requiring a code deployment.
- [Feature Request](/glossary/feature-request) — A suggestion from a user, customer, or stakeholder for new or improved product functionality.
- [Filter](/glossary/filter) — Criteria applied to narrow the set of visible items in backlogs, the Everything page, timelines, or reports.

## I

- [In Progress (Workflow Category)](/glossary/in-progress-workflow-category) — A workflow category containing steps representing active work.
- [Integration](/glossary/integration) — A connection between Atono and an external tool or service that synchronizes data or triggers actions across systems.

## K

- [Kanban](/glossary/kanban) — A team workflow type focused on continuous flow rather than time-boxed iterations.

## L

- [Linked Items](/glossary/linked-items) — Stories, bugs, or epics explicitly connected to each other to show relationships like dependencies or related work.

## M

- [Milestone](/glossary/milestone) — A significant checkpoint marking a key delivery or decision point in a product plan, such as a public launch, a regulatory deadline, or a major feature release.

## O

- [OKR](/glossary/okr) — Objectives and Key Results, a goal-setting framework linking qualitative objectives (what to achieve) to measurable key results (how success is measured).

## P

- [Priority](/glossary/priority) — A relative ranking of work items indicating their order of importance or urgency.
- [Product Backlog](/glossary/product-backlog) — An ordered list of all work known to be needed in the product, maintained and prioritized by the product owner.
- [Product Goal](/glossary/product-goal) — A long-term, outcome-oriented objective that provides direction for the product team's work across multiple sprints or releases.
- [Product Owner](/glossary/product-owner) — The Scrum role responsible for maximizing the value of the product by managing, ordering, and communicating the contents of the product backlog.

## R

- [Release](/glossary/release) — A key delivery milestone represented by a date, created as release markers on timelines.
- [Retrospective](/glossary/retrospective) — A recurring ceremony held at the end of each sprint where the Scrum team reflects on how they worked together and identifies specific improvements to carry into the next sprint.
- [Risk](/glossary/risk) — A potential future event or condition that could negatively affect the delivery or quality of a story, sprint, or release.
- [Roadmap](/glossary/roadmap) — A strategic visual plan showing the planned sequence and timing of product work across epics, releases, and themes.
- [Roles and Permissions](/glossary/roles-and-permissions) — Atono's multi-level access control system including workspace roles (Workspace Owner, Administrator, Product Manager, Standard User), team roles (Team Member, Team Administrator, Backlog Owner), and separate feature flag and glossary permissions.

## S

- [Scrum](/glossary/scrum) — A team workflow type focused on time-boxed iterations called sprints.
- [Severity](/glossary/severity) — The degree of impact a bug has on users or core system functionality, independent of how likely users are to encounter it.
- [Sprint](/glossary/sprint) — A time-boxed iteration for Scrum teams, typically one to four weeks, with defined start and end dates and a committed set of stories working toward a sprint goal.
- [Sprint Backlog](/glossary/sprint-backlog) — The set of product backlog items selected for a sprint, combined with the team's plan for delivering them toward the sprint goal.
- [Sprint Goal](/glossary/sprint-goal) — A single, concise objective the Scrum team commits to achieving by the end of a sprint, set during sprint planning.
- [Sprint Planning](/glossary/sprint-planning) — A Scrum ceremony at the start of each sprint where the team selects stories from the backlog and commits to a sprint goal.
- [Sprint Review](/glossary/sprint-review) — A Scrum ceremony held at the end of each sprint where the team demonstrates completed work to stakeholders and gathers feedback.
- [SSO](/glossary/sso) — Single sign-on, an authentication method allowing users to sign in to Atono using their organization's identity provider instead of email-based login links.
- [Stakeholder](/glossary/stakeholder) — A user with a critical team role (Team Administrator or Backlog Owner) who is not a team member.
- [Story](/glossary/story) — The core unit of user-focused work in Atono, capturing what needs to be built and why.
- [Story Points](/glossary/story-points) — A numerical measure derived from story size (XXS=1, XS=2, S=3, M=5, L=8, XL=13, XXL=21) using Fibonacci numbers.
- [Story Status](/glossary/story-status) — The current position of a story within a team's workflow, represented by a workflow step such as To Do, In Progress, In Review, or Done.
- [Subtask](/glossary/subtask) — An implementation step within a story or bug with title, optional description (up to 1000 chars with rich text), assignee, and status (To do, In progress, Done, Won't do).

## T

- [Team](/glossary/team) — A group of users organized to plan and deliver work together in Atono, using either Scrum or Kanban workflow.
- [Team Member](/glossary/team-member) — A team role granted automatically when a user joins a public or private team.
- [Timeline](/glossary/timeline) — A visual schedule of timeboxes created and owned by a user, showing how work is organized over time.
- [To do (Workflow Category)](/glossary/to-do-workflow-category) — A workflow category containing one step representing work ready to start but not yet active.
- [Triage](/glossary/triage) — The process of reviewing newly created bugs or issues to assess their severity, priority, and validity before they enter active development.

## U

- [User Story](/glossary/user-story) — A brief, structured description of a feature from an end user's perspective, written in the format: 'As a [persona], I want to [action] so that [benefit].

## V

- [Velocity](/glossary/velocity) — The average number of story points a Scrum team completes per sprint, calculated using only the team's most recent completed sprints within the configured velocity averaging window.

## W

- [Webhook](/glossary/webhook) — An HTTP callback that sends real-time event data from Atono to an external URL when a specified event occurs, such as a story being created, updated, or moved to a new workflow step.
- [Won't do (Workflow Category)](/glossary/wont-do-workflow-category) — A workflow category containing one step for work that will not be completed.
- [Workflow](/glossary/workflow) — A customizable sequence of 4–25 named steps that stories and bugs move through from ready-to-start to completion.
- [Workflow Category](/glossary/workflow-category) — One of four organizational groupings for workflow steps: To do (1 step), In Progress (1+ steps, contributes to cycle time), Done (1+ steps), Won't do (1 step).
